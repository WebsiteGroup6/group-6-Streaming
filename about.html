TYPES OF STREAMING
Streaming comes in different forms based on what you want to watch or listen to.
AUDIO STREAMING

Audio streaming features music platforms like Spotify for listening to songs and podcast services like Apple Podcasts for streaming talk shows or stories. In gaming, cloud gaming allows you to play games streamed from the internet, while live game streaming lets gamers broadcast their gameplay on platforms like Twitch.

VIDEO STREAMING

Streaming includes on-demand services like Netflix, where you can watch movies and shows whenever you want, and live streaming for events like sports games that happen in real-time.

SCREENSHARE/LIVE STREAMING

Sharing is useful in online meetings, enabling you to share your screen, as seen in apps like Zoom. Social media streaming allows you to go live and share videos with your followers on platforms like Facebook and Instagram. Webinar streaming involves attending online workshops or seminars, like those offered on Webex. Finally, interactive streaming lets viewers participate or make choices in the content, much like some game shows. Each type caters to different interests and delivers content in real-time or on-demand.
